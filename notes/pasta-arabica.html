The dish draws on some traditional and modern flavors that I‚Äôve encountered in Middle Eastern food.

The recipe is published here upon request. We enjoyed it, and I hope you enjoy it too!

Ingredients

These ingredients makes 2 large servings or 3-4 moderate servings.

Ingredient Amount
Garlic 1 bulb
Onion 1/2
Orange 1/2
Olive oil 2 Tbsp
Tahini 2 Tbsp
Ground beef/lamb 1/2 lb
Balsamic vinegar 1 Tbsp
Dry pasta ~1 1/3 cup
Pomegranate molasses 3 Tbsp
Kosher salt 2-3 Tbsp
Za‚Äôatar 2 Tbsp
Feta cheese 4 Tbsp

Prep

  1. Peel & dice the garlic
  2. Peel & dice 1/4 of the onion and slice the other 1/4
  3. Peel the orange, then peel the skin off the wedges of half the orange
  4. Bring 2-3 cups of water to boil on the side, ideally in a kettle

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil on medium heat
  2. Once hot, add in the dice garlic and onion. Fry until fragrant
  3. Add the ground beef and use a cooking utensil to separate it into small pieces
  4. Toss in peeled orange wedges and tahini, mixing until the tahini is evenly distributed
  5. Once the beef is cooked, add in balsamic vinegar to deglaze the pan
  6. Add in pasta and just enough water to submerge it. Set a timer for the amount of time needed to cook the pasta
  7. Stir in sliced onions, pomegranate molasses, and 2 Tbsp kosher salt
  8. Stir occasionally until pasta is cooked, adding boiling water as needed if the water has all boiled off. Season to desired saltiness.
  9. After the pasta is cooked, reduce liquid to desired viscosity and serve in a bowl. Top with za‚Äôatar and feta cheese.
